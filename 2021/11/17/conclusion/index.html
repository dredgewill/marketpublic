Conclusion

2021-11-17

Clearly, many countries' economies (or at least those we observed) are connected. We now know some explicit reasons why some of them are connected (like Australia and China, or proximity between Europe and Britain), but overall, I think it is better to see the ups and downs of markets as a global movement. Certainly, political factors, history, and government meddling aside, we are looking at indices, and what most affects the prices of indices (on the short term) are just people. And in our connected global economy today, what happens to the housing market in the United States may as well affect the banking industry in Australia.
